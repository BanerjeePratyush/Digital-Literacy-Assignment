**Project Report: Digital Literacy Infographic**

For this task, I used Canva to design a comprehensive visual mind map focusing on the core pillars of digital literacy. I chose a minimalist "sticky note" aesthetic to organize the information into digestible chunks, ensuring that the viewer can quickly grasp the key takeaways for each category.

My design covers **five critical areas**: the definition of digital literacy, useful student tools (like Google Scholar and Slack), safe internet practices, professional online presence, and proper email etiquette. By using a central hub labeled "Digital Literacy" with connecting arrows, I intended to show how these different skills are interconnected. For instance, maintaining a professional online presence on platforms like GitHub is a direct application of digital literacy.

One thing I found interesting while making this was the process of condensing broad topics like internet safety into just three essential bullet points: security, privacy and ethics. It was a challenge to maintain visual balance while ensuring the text remained large enough to read. Selecting the right icons, such as the puzzle pieces and the lightbulb, helped reinforce the idea that digital literacy is about putting together various skills to solve problems in a modern environment.
