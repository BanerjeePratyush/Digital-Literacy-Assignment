Task 4: Digital Communication Report
In this task, I practiced professional online communication. A common situation where poor digital communication causes significant problems is when a student sends an overly informal email to a professor, such as "hey, i can't finish the work, help." Because this email lacks a clear subject line, a formal greeting, and specific details (like the student's ID or the reason for the request), it often leads to the request being ignored or delayed, causing the student to miss important deadlines.

To avoid this, the communication should have been handled differently by following professional etiquette. By using a clear subject line like "Extension Request," a formal salutation, and a concise explanation of the issue, as demonstrated in my drafted emails, the student shows respect for the recipient's time. This professional approach ensures clearer communication and builds a better relationship between the student and faculty. Mastering these skills is essential for my future career in software development.
