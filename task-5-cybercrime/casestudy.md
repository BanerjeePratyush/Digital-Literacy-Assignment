# Case Study: UPI and Online Payment Fraud

UPI and online payment fraud is a rapidly growing cybercrime in India where attackers exploit the speed of real-time transactions to steal funds through social engineering. This crime typically begins with an attacker identifying a target on digital marketplaces or social media platforms. The step-by-step process usually starts with the fraudster posing as a legitimate buyer or a customer care executive to build a false sense of trust. They then send a "Payment Request" link or a QR code to the victim via WhatsApp or SMS, cleverly claiming that the victim must scan the code or click the link to "receive" a payment or a refund. In the heat of the moment, the victim is pressured to enter their secret UPI PIN. The moment the PIN is entered, the transaction is authorized, and instead of receiving money, the victim's bank account is instantly debited. This money is then quickly moved through a series of "mule accounts" to make it nearly impossible for authorities to trace. College students and small-scale online sellers are the primary targets because they perform frequent digital transactions and are often lured by the promise of quick payments. The consequences of such fraud are severe, often involving the loss of entire semester fees or personal savings, leading to immense mental stress and a long-term fear of using digital banking services. Recovery of these funds is extremely difficult unless the incident is reported to the 1930 helpline or the National Cyber Crime Portal within the "Golden Hour" of the theft.
