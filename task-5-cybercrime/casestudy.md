# Case Study: UPI and Online Payment Fraud

UPI and online payment fraud represents a rapidly growing cybercrime in India. Fraudsters exploit the speed of real-time transactions to steal funds through sophisticated social engineering techniques.
The process typically begins when attackers identify potential victims on digital marketplaces, classifieds, or social media platforms. Posing as a legitimate buyer, an Army officer, or a senior customer care executive, they establish a false sense of trust and authority. They then send a “Payment Request” link or a malicious QR code via WhatsApp or SMS, claiming that the victim must scan the code or click the link to receive a pending payment, prize, or government refund.
To intensify pressure, the fraudster often remains on a voice call, urging the victim to complete the process quickly. In the resulting confusion, the victim is tricked into entering their UPI PIN on what appears to be a standard payment interface. Once the PIN is entered, the transaction is authorised, resulting in an immediate debit from the victim’s bank account instead of a credit.
The stolen funds are promptly fragmented and routed through multiple “mule accounts” across different states, making tracing and recovery extremely difficult. College students and small-scale online sellers are the primary targets, as they frequently engage in digital transactions and are often enticed by promises of quick payments or attractive deals.
Fraudsters exploit the widespread misconception that a UPI PIN is required to receive money, whereas it is used only to send money. The consequences are severe, often resulting in the loss of semester fees, monthly allowances, or personal savings. Victims experience significant mental stress, long-term financial hardship, and reluctance to use digital banking services in the future.
Recovery of funds is rare and challenging unless the incident is reported immediately to the 1930 national helpline or the Cyber Crime Portal within the critical “Golden Hour.”
