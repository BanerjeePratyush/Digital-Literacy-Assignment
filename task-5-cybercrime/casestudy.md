# Case Study: UPI and Online Payment Fraud

UPI fraud is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick victims into sending money or revealing their PIN through deceptive digital transactions. This crime typically happens through social engineering. First, the attacker contacts the victim via phone or WhatsApp, often posing as a customer care executive or a buyer on a marketplace like OLX. 

The step-by-step process usually involves the "Request Money" feature. The attacker sends a payment request link to the victim, claiming that clicking it will allow the victim to *receive* money. In reality, once the victim enters their UPI PIN, the money is debited from their account instead of credited. Another common method is through malicious QR codes sent over chat.

College students and elderly people are usually targeted due to their frequent use of digital payments or lack of technical awareness. The consequences are often devastating, ranging from the immediate loss of semester fees or personal savings to severe emotional distress and a loss of trust in digital banking systems. Financial recovery is difficult unless the fraud is reported within the "Golden Hour" (the first two hours) to the authorities.
